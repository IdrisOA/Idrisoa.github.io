Hi, welcome to my website!

My research interest is focused on applying statistical methods (ranging from machine learning algorithms to survival analysis) for topics in statistical genetics and genomics.
I would like to become a Professor of Biostatistics in the future, and actively contributing to the design and analysis of studies that address pressing global health issues.
Concentrating on Biostatistics at the Doctorate level will bring me a step closer to acheiving my long-term goal of being a researcher.
I also aspire to collaborate with multidisciplinary researchers to translate statistical findings into actionable insights, thereby influencing health policies and interventions.

My Background

I studied Statistics as an undergraduate at the University of Nigeria, Nsukka where I graduated with a Bachelor's degree in Statistics advised by Dr. Eugene C. Ukaegbu.
My first exposure to Biostatistics was during my internship at the National Agency for Food and Drug Administration & Control where I worked as a Research Assistant, since then,
I've been contributing and improving on my skillsets in the biomedical area of statistics.

More so, my interest in statistical genetics and genomics started during my undergraduate research thesis, where I investigated cancer patients and cancer-related death cases
at the University of Nigeria Teaching Hospital (UNTH), Enugu State, Nigeria using Two-Way CATANOVA.
