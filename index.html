Research

My research interest center around applying statistical
methods (ranging from machine learning algorithms to
survival analysis) for topics in statistical genetics and
genomics research, high-dimensional data and statistical
learning. In my head, the big goal is to develop
Machine Learning models that addresses clinical trial
problems, understanding high-dimensional genomics
data and other evolving health related challenges.

My ultimate future goal is to be a Biostatistics Professor,
conducting quality research, publishing papers in top
reputable journals, and contributing to the design and
analysis of studies that addresses pressing global health
issues. I would like to collaborate with multidisciplinary
researchers, to translate statistical findings into
actionable insights, thereby influencing health
policies and interventions.

Background

I studied Statistics at the University of Nigeria as an
undergraduate where I graduated with a Bachelor's degree.
During my undergrad thesis, I investigated cancer patients
and cancer-related death cases in Nigeria: a case study
of Abuja and Ibadan cancer registries centers
advised by Dr. Eugene Ukaegbu.

My first exposure to biostatistics began during my intern
at National Agency for Food and Drug (NAFDAC)
where I worked as a Research Assistant, since that time;
I have been contributing and upskilling in the
biomedical area of Statistics. My interest in
statistical genetics & genomics research began
during my undergraduate thesis.

I am currently open to research collaborations.
