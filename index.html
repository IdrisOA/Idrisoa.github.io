Hi, welcome to my website!

Research

My research interest center around applying statistical methods(ranging from Machine Learning algorithms to Survival Analysis)
for topics in statistical genetics and genomics.
In my head, the big goal is to leverage statistical machine learning
to address health related and clinical trial problems.
I aspire to be a Professor of Biostatistics
in the future contributing to the design and analysis of studies
that addresses pressing global health issues.
A Doctoral level Biostatistics concentration will bring me close to
acheiving my long-term goal of being a Research Scientist.
I would also like to collaborate with multidisciplinary researchers to translate statistical findings
into actionable insights, thereby influencing health policies and interventions.

Background

I studied Statistics as an undergraduate at the University of Nigeria
where I graduated with a Bachelor's degree in Statistics advised by Dr. Eugene C. Ukaegbu.
My first exposure to Biostatistics was during my internship at
the National Agency for Food and Drug Administration & Control where I worked as a Research Assistant,
since then, I've been contributing and improving on my skillsets in the biomedical area of statistics.

More so, my interest in statistical genetics and genomics started
during my undergraduate research thesis, where I investigated
cancer patients and cancer-related death cases
at the University of Nigeria Teaching Hospital (UNTH) using Two-Way CATANOVA
design and analysis of experiment.
