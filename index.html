Research

My research interest center around applying statistical
methods (ranging from machine learning algorithms to
survival analysis) for topics in statistical genetics
and genomics.
In my head, the big goal is to leverage statistical machine
learning in addressing clinical trial challenges and health
related problems.

My ultimate goal is to be a Professor of Biostatistics,
conduct quality research and publish papers in reputable
journals, while contributing to the design and analysis
of studies that addresses pressing global health issues.
I would like to collaborate with multidisciplinary
researchers, to translate statistical findings into
actionable insights, thereby influencing health policies
and interventions.

Background

I studied Statistics at the University of Nigeria
as an undergraduate student where I graduated with
a Bachelor's degree in Statistics
advised by Dr. Mbanefo Madukaife.

My first exposure to Biostatistics began during my intern
at National Agency for Food and Drug (NAFDAC)
where I worked as a Research Assistant,
since then, I've been contributing and upskilling in the
Biomedical aspect of Statistics.

My interest in statistical genetics and genomics research
spiked during my undergraduate thesis,
where I investigated cancer patients and
cancer-related death cases at the
University of Nigeria Teaching Hospital (UNTH)
advised by Dr. Eugene Ukaegbu.
