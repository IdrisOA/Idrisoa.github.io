Hi, welcome to my website!

Research

My research interest center around applying statistical
methods (ranging from machine learning algorithms to
survival analysis) for topics in statistical genetics
and genomics.
In my head, the big goal is to leverage statistical machine
learning to address health related and clinical trial
problems.
I aspire to be a Professor of Biostatistics in the future
contributing to the design and analysis of studies
that addresses pressing global health issues.
A Doctoral level Biostatistics concentration will bring me
close to acheiving my long-term goal of being
a Research Scientist.
I would also like to collaborate with multidisciplinary
researchers to translate statistical findings into actionable insights,
thereby influencing health policies and interventions.

Background

I studied Statistics at the University of Nigeria
as an undergraduate student where I graduated with
a Bachelor's degree in Statistics
advised by Dr. Eugene C. Ukaegbu and Dr. Mbanefo Madukaife.

My first exposure to Biostatistics was during my internship at the
National Agency for Food and Drug Administration & Control
where I worked as a Research Assistant, since then, I've been
contributing and improving on my skillsets in the
biomedical aspect of statistics.

In addition, my interest in statistical genetics and genomics started
during my undergraduate research thesis, where I investigated
cancer patients and cancer-related death cases at the
University of Nigeria Teaching Hospital (UNTH) using
Two-Way CATANOVA experimental designs.
